## Final Framing

### RQ2

On **open-ended personality-sensitive tasks**, where do switch effects show up after changing from persona A to persona B?

Current evidence source:

- `Machine Mindset`
- preferred evaluation sets:
  - `data/processed/machine_mindset_self_awareness_eval.jsonl`
  - `data/processed/machine_mindset_dimension_eval.jsonl`

### RQ3

On **constrained instruction-following tasks**, does persona switching still affect instruction-following behavior after the prompt is updated to persona B?

Current evidence source:

- `IFEval`
- `data/processed/ifeval_full.parquet`

Current experimental control design:

- `B_only`
- `A_history_to_B`
- `A_summary_to_B`
- `B_history_to_B`
- `B_summary_to_B`

Interpretation focus:

- strict instruction-following pass rate
- strict instruction coverage
- deltas versus `B_only`
- paired comparison against `B_history_to_B` and `B_summary_to_B`

## Why This Revision

The original proposal phrased `RQ3` as a direct comparison between open-ended and constrained tasks. In practice, the current `Machine Mindset` and `IFEval` metrics are not on a perfectly shared scale:

- `IFEval` has an official checker
- `Machine Mindset` uses a labeled reference-bank alignment method

Because of this, the cleaner and more defensible analysis is:

- `RQ2` = open-ended task analysis
- `RQ3` = constrained task analysis

rather than forcing a strong cross-task numerical comparison.

Within each task family, the current preferred design also controls for the retention mechanism itself:

- `B_only` gives the no-memory baseline
- `B_history_to_B` and `B_summary_to_B` isolate the effect of retaining prior B-context
- `A_history_to_B` and `A_summary_to_B` test whether prior A adds extra carry-over beyond that mechanism-only effect
